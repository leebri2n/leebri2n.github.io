Transfer Learning on GANs

Brian Lee, Logan Eisenbeiser, VTNSI (2022)

ITEA Article

Background

Ever since their introduction in 2014 by Ian Goodfellow and colleagues, generative adversarial networks (GANs) have been a popular subject in the field of generative modeling. The GAN framework consists of two networks. A generator is tasked with generating a plausible sample while a discriminator is tasked with classifying if a given sample is from the generator or a real sample from a given dataset.

Although this structure plays out in theory, in practice, the training process is often lengthy and costly, with numerous training pitfalls resulting in failed generation behavior that persists. Both a sufficient training time and quantity of training data are required. In circumstances that lack an abundance of samples or computational constraints, it would be helpful to have GANs able to trian quickly and without absurd amounts of data. This project

In this project, we aim to use transfer learning as a potential method to train GANs on relatively small datasets. Using the FID score as a performance metric, we will observe how viable and efficient this trianing method will be.
