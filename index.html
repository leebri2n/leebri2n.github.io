RESEARCH

Transfer Learning in GAN Training

Training effective generative adversarial networks (GANs) from scratch can often be a challenge. Not only is the training process costly in time and computational resources, but datasets of even slightly insufficient sizes encounter siginificant training failures.

This project evaluates both the efficacy and efficiency of transfer learning as a viable method of training generative adversarial networks (GANs) on limited datasets of images assembled from Instagram. A paper on my results submitted to the ITEA Journal of Test and Evaluation is currently pending preapproval.

Combinatorial Coverage for Dataset Exploration Tool

Model generalization is often a primary concern when applying a trained model beyond the dataset on which it was trained. Often overlooked in a dataset is how its features interact with one another as they are either present or absent, as captured the combinatorial coverage metric.

This project aimed to create a tool that integrates splitting of the dataset, model training and testing, and visualizes the relationship between combinatorial coverage and model performance.

Comparing Latent Space Representations of Protein Sequence Models

To understand the special relationship between a protein's amino acid sequence and its function, bioengineers of today have turned to machine learning-based approaches to model this relationship. While models exhibit excellent predictive power, their outputs lack interpretability, failing to retain the biological semantics being learned from amino acid sequences.

This CMDA capstone project under the sponsorship of the National Institute of Standards and technology and completed by me and three other CMDA undergraduate teammates explores whether or not latent representations of protein sequences learned by machine learning models are similar or different.

Alex Ovechkin Goal-Scoring Exploratory Data Analysis and Visualization

Washington Capitals elite Alexander Ovechkin has just taken second place for all-time career goals scored in NHL history and shows no sign of slowing down. Although it is obvious he has a talent for scoring goals, the game data suggests that he is in a league of his own entirely.

In this early and very personal project, I use basic data preprocessing and statistical methods to extract characteristics and patterns in Ovechkin's goal-scoring. They are subsequently used in striking and creative visualizations to easily convey his unmatched proficency when compared to his peers leaguewide.
